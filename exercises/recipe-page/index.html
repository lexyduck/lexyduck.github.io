ENTRCOTE STEAK SAUCE

One of my absolute favorite things to eat in France was steak frites with this sauce. I loved it so much that I had to figure out how to recreate it at home. This version stays true to what I ate there while still being doable in your own kitchen.

PREP TIME: 15 MINUTES      COOK TIME 15 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 30 MINUTES      YIELDS: 1 CUP SAUCE

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 shallot, finely minced
  • 1½ sticks unsalted butter (170 grams), cold and cubed, divided
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3 to 4 basil leaves
  • Small handful tarragon
  • 4 sage leaves
  • 1 handful chervil
  • 2 anchovies, bones removed
  • 6 capers
  • 2 tablespoons warm beef broth
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg

INSTRUCTIONS

    1. In a small pan over medium heat, add 2 tablespoons of the butter and let it melt. Add the minced shallot and cook gently until soft and translucent, without browning.
    2. Once the shallots are done, remove the pan from the heat. Add the remaining butter cubes directly to the pan and stir gently, letting the residual heat melt the butter slowly until glossy. Set aside to cool slightly.
    3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and lemon juice until smooth.
    4. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and lemon juice until smooth.
    5. Transfer the yolk mixture to a blender or food processor. Add the basil, tarragon, sage, chervil, anchovies, and capers. Blend until completely smooth and very thick, like a mayonnaise.
    6. With the blender running on low, slowly add the butter and shallot mixture in three additions, blending well between each addition until fully incorporated and silky.
    7. Add the warm beef broth and blend briefly just until combined.
    8. Season with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Blend once more to combine.
    9. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. The sauce should be creamy, glossy, smooth, and spoonable.
    10. Serve immediately over sliced steak or entrecôte, ideally with fries.
